# Color Variable Naming Convention

This naming convention is designed to ensure consistency and readability for color variables.
The convention follows the structure: `--<category>-<intensity>-<name>-<state>`.

## Structure

1. **Category**: Indicates the general category of the color.
Examples: `background`, `text`, `border`

2. **Intensity**: Specifies the specific intensity or variant within the type.
Examples: `subtle`, `moderate`, `emphasis`

3. **Name**: Specifies the name of color usage.
Examples: `primary`, `secondary`, `tertiary`, `brand`, `success`, `error`, `warning`, `yellow`, `purple`

4. **State**: Specifies the state of the color.
Examples: `default`, `hover`, `disabled`


## Examples

1. **Background Colors**
- bg.subtle.primary.default becomes `--bg-subtle-primary-default`
- bg.moderate.secondary.hover becomes `--bg-moderate-secondary-hover`
- bg.emphasis.brand.disabled becomes `--bg-emphasis-brand-disabled`

2. **Text Colors**
- text.emphasis.primary.default becomes `--text-emphasis-primary-default`
- text.subtle.secondary.hover becomes `--text-subtle-secondary-hover`
- text.moderate.brand.disabled becomes `--text-moderate-brand-disabled`

3. **Border Colors**
- border.subtle.primary.default becomes `--border-subtle-primary-default`
- border.moderate.success.hover becomes `--border-moderate-success-hover`
- border.emphasis.error.disabled becomes `--border-emphasis-error-disabled`

## Full Example List

```typescript
--bg-subtle-primary-default: 'var(--color-neutral-0, #FFF)';
--bg-subtle-primary-hover: 'var(--color-neutral-100, #F6F7F9)';
--bg-subtle-primary-disabled: 'var(--color-neutral-200, #EDEFF3)';

--bg-emphasis-brand-default: 'var(--color-brand-500, #0673F9)';
--bg-emphasis-brand-hover: 'var(--color-brand-400, #2989FF)';
--bg-emphasis-brand-disabled: 'var(--color-brand-200, #94C4FF)';

--text-emphasis-primary-default: 'var(--color-neutral-900, #16191D)';
--text-emphasis-primary-disabled: 'var(--color-neutral-600, #8C95A6)';

--border-subtle-primary-default: 'var(--color-neutral-300, #E1E5EA)';
--border-subtle-primary-disabled: 'var(--color-neutral-200, #EDEFF3)';
```

This naming convention ensures that each variable name is descriptive and follows a consistent pattern, making it easier to understand and maintain.
